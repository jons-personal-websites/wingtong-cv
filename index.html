Case Study

Redesigning the POS management experience for multi-location restaurants

Designer & Product Manager · F&B Technology

Overview

A central POS management dashboard web app enabling IT executives to update menu and point-of-sale configurations across multiple restaurant locations. The client operates 20 quick-service restaurant chains in Singapore.

The Problem

Inefficient Menu Updates: Required multiple steps to update menu data per outlet per product. When price adjustments affected multiple locations, executives updated each outlet individually. CSV imports required separate files per category per outlet.

Inflexible Update Timing: Any action on the dashboard took immediate effect, forcing updates only before business hours or at midnight.

Offline Accessibility Issues: Web-based system became unavailable when cashiers refreshed pages offline, with intermittent internet creating escalations.

The Solution

Master Categories: Update product lists across outlets simultaneously instead of one-by-one.

Localised Pricing: Each product displays its default price with all outlets. Outlets can override the default or hide products. Only overridden outlets are shown.

Manual Sync Control: Universal Sync Button appears when unsynced POSs exist, with bulk actions for multiple devices.

Outcomes

Eliminates CSV imports and reduces menu management effort. Planned metrics: CSAT, time tracking for updates, escalation reduction.

Case Study

Optimising the checkout process for quick service restaurants

Sole Designer · Experience design, visual interface, design system

Overview

An Android POS app for quick service restaurant cashiers to take orders and process payment. Transitioning from cloud-based to a hybrid POS system storing data locally while syncing to the cloud.

The Problem

Limited Customisation: Only one product variant could be recorded, preventing multiple modifications like "no chilli".

Interface Usability: Quantity stepper located far from cart items and close to clear cart button, causing accidental presses.

Payment Screen Confusion: Pay, clear, and cancel buttons too close together.

Receipt Discovery: Cashiers had to key in exact receipt numbers to locate receipts.

Accessibility: Majority of cashiers had Chinese as native language with limited English literacy. Truncated product names, untranslated errors, small fonts.

The Solution

Multiple Customisations: One-page scroll option (fewer taps), optional with defaults.

Simplified Cart Controls: Quantity stepper relocated to individual cart items, swipe to delete.

Display Labels: Physical counter numbers on product cards.

Payment Optimization: Voucher button relocated to payment screen, "exact" button for precise amounts.

Receipt Browsing: Changed from search to browse mode with daily receipt list.

Design System: Comprehensive system developed during high-fidelity phases.

Outcomes

Decreased Avg Transaction Time

Decreased User Error Rate

Case Study

Helping accountants upskill with courses

Experience & Interface Designer · Institute of Singapore Chartered Accountants

Overview

A redesign for the Institute of Singapore Chartered Accountants (ISCA) — Singapore's accountancy body with over 32,000 members. Corporate website redesign and app prototype completed within seven months.

The Problem

Poor Search Relevance: Results sorted alphabetically, overwhelming filters, calendar view on separate page.

Poor Wayfinding: Deep navigation, confusing terminology ("capability development", "CPE training"), excessive weekly inquiries.

Irrelevant Content for Non-Members: Website tailored to existing members, not prospective accountants.

Lengthy Add-to-Cart Process: Course-specific declarations plus personal details forms, cumbersome with SSG/SFC funding.

The Solution

Flattened IA: Reduced from 7 to 3 layers, renamed ambiguous terminology.

Improved Search: Based on 15 interviews — top criteria: topic/keyword, date availability, CPE hours. Added calendar view alongside list view.

Persona-Based Segmentation: Four user types (junior, experienced, students, private). Added persona filter on homepage.

Streamlined Purchase: Combined date and funding selection, moved declarations to checkout, auto-fill from profile.

Case Study

Building an emotional connection for a gateway & catering brand

Lead Designer · Team of 7, 7-month project

Overview

A corporate website redesign for an aviation, healthcare, and hospitality services company operating across 55+ locations.

The Problem

Scalability Issues: Existing site couldn't scale with hypergrowth.

Brand Communication Gap: Needed to communicate expansion into gateway and catering services.

Poor Navigation: Navigation for services and global contacts was poor.

The Solution

People-First Storytelling: Foregrounded staff through authentic articles, videos, photos, and emotional messaging.

Simplified Service Architecture: Tested two-way filter (business needs x industry sectors) but found it introduced friction. Reverted to straightforward two-category structure for 60+ services.

Case Study

Making trip planning to Science Centre a breeze

Interface Designer · Team with lead designer, PM, researcher, 3 developers

Overview

A scientific institution corporate website redesign expanding the target audience from parents to young adults, over a 6-month period.

The Problem

Information Overload: Content-heavy site made finding key exhibition info difficult. Some exhibits required advanced science literacy unsuitable for young children.

Outdated Visual Identity: Target audience shifted to include young adults, but design still targeted children.

The Solution

Itinerary Planner: Smart generator creating personalized itineraries based on visit duration, children's ages, and interests.

Interactive Map: SVG-based 2D map with exhibition pins for building navigation.

Visual Rebrand: Retained orange, introduced dark blue, refined typography, immersive photography for young adults.

Outcomes

Improved visit planning efficiency across all audience segments.
